# Student Discipline Regulations (Technion)

The **Student Disciplinary Code (תקנון משמעתי לסטודנטים)** governs all disciplinary matters at the Technion. It entered into force on **10 February 2016** (1 Adar I 5776), as approved and amended by the Technion Senate in meetings on 10 May 2015, 22 November 2015, and 30 December 2018.

> "Studies at the Technion are based on trust between the institution and those who study in it. Students are obligated to academic integrity and to conduct befitting the honor of the Technion, its students, staff, and faculty."

---

## Scope and Definitions

### Who is a "Student" (§1.2)

The disciplinary code applies to anyone who, at the time of the offense, was:
- (a) Registered at the Technion, including those in the admission process whose application has not yet been rejected
- (b) Studying at the Technion even while on academic leave or suspension
- (c) Completed their studies but has not yet been awarded their degree or certificate
- (d) Completed studies and received their degree, if conviction of the charged offense could lead to revocation of that degree

The code covers students in the School of Undergraduate Studies, the Graduate School, the Continuing Education School, the Pre-Academic Education Center, and any other Technion-run framework (unless that framework has its own independent disciplinary code).

---

## Disciplinary Rules (Chapter 3)

Every student is obligated to:

| Rule | Obligation |
|------|-----------|
| §3.1 | Uphold rules of integrity, fairness, and ethics in all academic activity |
| §3.2 | Faithfully fulfill all academic and other obligations imposed by Technion statutes and procedures |
| §3.3 | Respect the Technion and its institutions, the academic/technical/administrative staff, and the student body; conduct oneself appropriately |
| §3.4 | Follow instructions given by Technion personnel in the course of their duties |
| §3.5 | Protect Technion property and maintain cleanliness and order on campus |

---

## Disciplinary Offenses (Chapter 4)

### Academic Integrity Offenses (§4.1)

A student commits a disciplinary offense by breaching an academic obligation, including:

| Sub-section | Offense |
|-------------|---------|
| §4.1.1 | Acting through fraud or other illegitimate means to improve a grade on an exam, quiz, paper, project, practical training, or any other academic task — or to gain any academic advantage — for oneself or for another student |
| §4.1.2 | Possessing unauthorized study material or equipment during an exam or quiz, whether or not it was actually used |
| §4.1.3 | Copying from another person, or knowingly allowing another person to copy from oneself, in an exam, quiz, paper, project, or any other academic task |
| §4.1.4 | Assisting another student or receiving assistance from another person during an exam, quiz, or other academic task that requires independent work |
| §4.1.5 | Conspiring or participating in a conspiracy to commit or attempt any of the above acts |

### Behavioral Offenses

| Section | Offense |
|---------|---------|
| §4.2 | Failing to comply with instructions given by Technion personnel in the course of their duties, or refusing to identify oneself to such personnel |
| §4.3 | Violating a regulation, instruction, or procedure (including those governing use of facilities/property or conduct rules whose violation is defined as a disciplinary offense) |
| §4.4 | Theft, unauthorized taking, damage, or injury to Technion property, property of Technion personnel, property of guests, or any business/commerce on campus |
| §4.5 | Knowingly harming the honor of the Technion, or the honor or good name of any Technion person or guest |
| §4.6 | Participating in a brawl, acting violently, attacking, or injuring any Technion person or guest while on campus or at a Technion activity (even if the activity is off-campus) |
| §4.7 | Disrupting or interfering with teaching, research, or work at the Technion, or any other activity on campus (except with prior approval from an authorized party) |
| §4.8 | Using Technion property or property of Technion personnel without authorization for private or non-Technion purposes |
| §4.9 | Breaking into Technion property or facilities, or otherwise trespassing on Technion grounds |
| §4.10 | Sexual harassment or victimization as defined in the Technion's sexual harassment prevention procedure; violations are handled under that procedure's special provisions |
| §4.11 | Fraud, deception, forgery, or providing false information in connection with student obligations or rights (one's own or another student's) |
| §4.12 | Disrupting (by act or omission) a disciplinary proceeding — including perjury, fabricating or destroying evidence, refusing to testify when summoned, or any other act intended to obstruct a disciplinary proceeding or cause a miscarriage of justice |
| §4.13 | Impersonating another Technion person (using a computer or otherwise) |

### Library Offenses (§4.14)

- Taking Technion library materials without authorization, failing to return them, defacing them, or removing pages (§4.14.1)
- Infringing copyright in materials held by the library (§4.14.2)

### Computer Offenses (§4.15)

A detailed list covering: unauthorized access to Technion computers, email accounts, or files; causing damage to data or software; using Technion computers for private/commercial purposes; copyright infringement via Technion systems; sharing access passwords; distributing hate speech or obscenity via Technion networks; cyberattacks using Technion systems; and violating the Computer Law (5755-1995).

### Traffic/Parking Offenses (§4.16)

Violations of traffic signs, dangerous driving on campus, unauthorized parking (including in disability parking spots), and illegally obtaining parking permits.

### Other Offenses

- §4.17: Violating scholarship conditions without authorization
- §4.18: Criminal conviction (final judgment) with a nexus to being a Technion student or which damages Technion's honor
- §4.19: Conduct unbecoming a Technion student in connection with being a student
- §4.20: Violating obligations arising from professional training (e.g., confidentiality or medical secrecy)

## Judicial Bodies (Chapter 5 and Chapter 6)

### Three Tiers

| Body | Composition | Role |
|------|-------------|------|
| **Disciplinary Tribunal (בית-הדין המשמעתי)** | Single judge (staff member) OR panel of 3 (2 staff + 1 student) | Primary hearing |
| **Single Judge (דן יחיד)** | One staff member (full professor, associate professor) | Simplified cases |
| **Appeals Tribunal (בית-הדין לערעורים)** | Panel of 3 (2 staff + 1 student) | Appeals |

### Judge Pool (§6.1)

- At least 6 staff judges for the Disciplinary Tribunal + 5 for the Appeals Tribunal
- 12 student judges: 7 from the undergraduate school, 5 from graduate school (who may serve on either tribunal)
- Staff judges appointed by the Central Committee with Senate approval; student judges appointed by the Senior Vice-President for Academic Affairs on the Student Union's recommendation
- Staff tenure: 2 years (renewable); Student tenure: 1 year (renewable)
- No judge may simultaneously hold appointment in both the Disciplinary Tribunal and the Appeals Tribunal

## Prosecution Procedure (Chapter 7)

1. **Complaint**: Any Technion person who becomes aware of a suspected disciplinary offense may report it to the **Prosecutor (תובע)**, who is appointed by the Senior Vice-President
2. **Investigation**: The Prosecutor reviews the relevant material and may dismiss the complaint or file charges
3. **Indictment (כתב קובלנה)**: If charges are filed, the indictment includes: the facts attributed to the defendant (including place and time), the applicable section from Chapter 4, the list of prosecution witnesses, and a recommendation whether to try before a single judge or a three-judge panel
4. **Service**: The indictment is served on the defendant, who is informed of the right to examine all documents held by the prosecution and to be assisted by a Technion person ("accompanier") during proceedings
5. **Timeline**: The Tribunal Secretary coordinates the hearing date, generally within **two months** of filing the indictment (hearings do not ordinarily take place during exam periods or summer vacation)

### Statute of Limitations (§7.12)

A student may not be tried for an offense if **three years** have elapsed from the date of the offense to the filing of the indictment — unless the expected punishment could involve revocation of a degree, in which case the limitation does not apply.

### Filing More Than One Year After the Offense (§7.11)

Filing an indictment more than one year after the offense requires approval from the Dean of Undergraduate Studies.

## Legal Representation (Chapter 8)

| Scenario | Right to Lawyer |
|----------|----------------|
| Default | Defendant may be accompanied by a non-lawyer Technion person ("מלווה") |
| If the Prosecutor is represented by a lawyer | Defendant automatically entitled to be represented by a lawyer of their choice at their own expense |
| Severe expected punishment (see below) or significant disadvantage | Tribunal may permit representation by a lawyer |
| In any case where a lawyer is permitted | Prosecutor may also be represented by a lawyer |

**"Severe punishments" (עונשים חמורים) per §8.3.2:**
- Cancellation of courses exceeding 30 credit points (§12.6)
- Expulsion or access restriction for more than one year (§§12.7, 12.8, 12.9)
- Retroactive revocation of a certificate or degree (§12.14)

---

## Plea Agreements (Chapter 9)

The Prosecutor and defendant may reach a plea agreement. The defendant must declare in writing awareness of the right to an accompanier. The agreement must be confirmed by:
- The Tribunal Chair (if no panel has yet been formed and no actual expulsion is included), or
- The full panel (if proceedings have already started or the agreement includes actual expulsion)

The Tribunal is not bound to accept the agreed sanction but will generally do so; it may deviate in exceptional circumstances with written reasons.

---

## Punishments (Chapter 12)

The Tribunal may impose one or more of the following:

| § | Punishment |
|---|-----------|
| §12.1 | Reprimand (נזיפה) |
| §12.2 | Grade of 0 on an exam, quiz, paper, project, lab, exercise, or other academic task |
| §12.3 | Grade of 0 on a specific component in a course's final grade weighting |
| §12.4 | Grade of 0 in an entire course |
| §12.5 | Revocation of: academic awards, excellence scholarships (including "Dean's List"/"President's List" status), study scholarships, dormitory rights, sports facility use, vehicle access, student exchange participation — for a set period |
| §12.6 | Cancellation of courses taken at the Technion (including courses for which the student received a waiver), from the semester in which the offense was committed, in whole or in part |
| §12.7 | Suspension from studies at the Technion for a set number of semesters (including the current semester) |
| §12.8 | Permanent expulsion from Technion studies |
| §12.9 | Restricted access to Technion grounds, for a set period or permanently |
| §12.10 | Community service at the Technion (security duty, library/lab/sports assistance); duration set in the verdict |
| §12.11 | Compensation for material damage to Technion property or Technion personnel — up to 3x the value of damage caused |
| §12.12 | Monetary fine not exceeding half of annual full-time tuition |
| §12.13 | Repayment of scholarship(s) or any portion thereof |
| §12.14 | Retroactive cancellation of a completion certificate, diploma, or degree — if the offense was committed in serious circumstances and the academic achievement was obtained illegitimately |
| §12.15 | Any other punishment or required action deemed reasonable and appropriate |

### Minimum Punishment for Grade-Improvement Offenses (§12.18)

If convicted of an act intended to improve a grade or obtain an academic advantage in an exam, quiz, or semesterly paper, the **minimum punishment** is the lowest punishment listed in §12.2 or §12.3 (as applicable) **AND** §12.7 (suspension), unless the Tribunal finds special written reasons not to impose this minimum.

### Suspended Sentences (§12.16)

Punishments under §§12.5, 12.6, 12.7, 12.9, 12.10, 12.12, and 12.13 may be imposed as **actual** or **suspended** sentences.

### Restrictions on Single Judge (§12.17)

A single judge may impose all punishments **except** §§12.6 (if exceeding 30 points), 12.8 (permanent expulsion), 12.14 (degree revocation), and 12.15 (unlimited); suspension under §12.7 may only be imposed as suspended, for one semester only; access restriction under §12.9 may be for a fixed term not exceeding one semester.

---

## Appeals (Chapter 13)

- Appeals must be filed within **14 days** of receiving the verdict/sentence
- Appeals are submitted in writing in two copies to the Tribunal Secretary
- The Appeals Tribunal does not ordinarily permit new evidence (requires special written reasons)
- The Appeals Tribunal may uphold, reduce, or annul the sentence; it may only **increase** the sentence if the Prosecutor appealed the leniency of the sentence
- Appeals Tribunal decisions are **final** (subject only to a pardon request to the President under §15)

---

## Pardon (Chapter 15)

A pardon request (בקשת חנינה) may be submitted to the Technion President:
- Generally, only after at least **one year** from the date the sentence entered into force
- Exception: If the Tribunal permits an immediate pardon request (for special written reasons), execution of the sentence is delayed 14 days
- The pardon may include cancellation or reduction of the punishment, with or without conditions
- The President decides after consulting the relevant Dean

## נתונים בעברית

**תקנון משמעתי לסטודנטים – בתוקף החל מיום 10.2.2016, א׳ אדר א׳ תשע״ו**
כפי שאושר ותוקן ע"י סנט הטכניון בישיבותיו מתאריכים 10.5.2015, 22.11.2015 ו-30.12.2018.

הלימודים בטכניון מבוססים על אמון בין המוסד לבין הלומדים בו. הסטודנטים מחויבים ליושרה אקדמית ולהתנהגות ההולמת את כבוד הטכניון, הסטודנטים, העובדים וחברי-הסגל האקדמי.

**עבירות משמעתיות עיקריות (פרק 4):**

§4.1.1 – עשה, בהונאה או בדרך בלתי כשרה אחרת, מעשה על מנת לשפר ציון בבחינה, בוחן, עבודה, פרויקט, השתלמות מעשית או כל מטלה לימודית אחרת, או על מנת להשיג יתרון אקדמי, עבורו או עבור סטודנט אחר.

§4.1.2 – החזיק ברשותו, חומר לימודי או ציוד אסור בעת בחינה או בוחן, בין אם השתמש בו במהלך הבחינה או הבוחן ובין אם לא.

§4.1.3 – העתיק מאחר, או איפשר ביודעין, לאחר, להעתיק ממנו בבוחן, בחינה, עבודה, פרויקט או בכל מטלה לימודית אחרת.

§4.12 – שיבש, במעשה או במחדל, הליך משמעתי, ולרבות: העיד שקר, בידה ראיות, השמיד ראיות, סירב להעיד הגם שזומן על ידי בית הדין.

**עונשים (פרק 12):**

§12.18 – הורשע סטודנט במעשה שיש בו כדי לשפר ציון או להשיג יתרון אקדמי עבורו או עבור סטודנט אחר בדרך לא כשרה והמעשה יש בו כדי להשפיע על הציון של הבחינה או של בחן או של עבודה סמסטריאלית, יהיה העונש המינימלי על עבירה כזו העונש הנמוך ביותר הנקוב בסעיפים 12.2 או 12.3 (לפי העניין) ו-12.7 אלא אם יקבע בית-הדין, מטעמים מיוחדים שיירשמו, כי אין להטיל את עונש המינימום הנ"ל.

**התיישנות (§7.12):**
לא יועמד סטודנט לדין משמעתי על עבירה, אם חלפו שלוש שנים מיום ביצוע העבירה ועד למועד הגשת כתב הקובלנה. על אף האמור לעיל, ההתיישנות לא תחול כאשר העונש הצפוי בעקבות הרשעה עשוי למנוע קבלה של אישור סיום לימודים או עשוי להביא לביטולם בדיעבד.
