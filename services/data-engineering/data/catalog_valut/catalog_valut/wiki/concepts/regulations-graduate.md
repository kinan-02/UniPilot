# Graduate Study Regulations — Technion Jacobs Graduate School

The Jacobs Graduate School (בית הספר לתארים מתקדמים ע"ש ג'ייקובס) administers all advanced-degree programs at the Technion. The regulations below are drawn from the official 2025–26 catalog (pages 42–72). In any conflict between this summary and the official regulations, the official regulations govern.

---

## 1. Graduate Degree Types

### Master's degrees (מגיסטר)

| Hebrew title | English title | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| מגיסטר למדעים (MSc) | Master of Science | Research track — requires thesis |
| מגיסטר להנדסה (ME) | Master of Engineering | Course-only track in engineering units |
| מגיסטר ל... / מגיסטר ב... | Master of ... | Approved non-engineering discipline tracks |
| מגיסטר במנהל עסקים (MBA) | Master of Business Administration | Tel Aviv campus; 80 credits |
| מגיסטר לארכיטקטורה (MArch) | Master of Architecture and Urbanism | Two variants (I and II) |
| מגיסטר להנדסה עירונית (MUE) | Master of Urban Engineering | |

#### Credit requirements per track

| Degree type | Minimum advanced credits | Thesis/project credits | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| MSc research track (4-year BSc) | 18–20 | 20 (thesis) | Total ~38–46 credits counted |
| MSc research track (3-year BSc) | 30–36 | 20 (thesis) | Total ~50–55 credits |
| ME / non-thesis track (4-year BSc) | 42 minimum | — | Courses only |
| Non-thesis track (3-year BSc) | 20+ supplementary + 42 advanced | — | |
| עבודת גמר (final project) track | 28 course credits | 12 (project) | Where approved by unit |
| PhD (regular track, after MSc) | 5 minimum | Dissertation | At least 5 advanced credits |
| PhD special track (4-year BSc) | 25 minimum | Dissertation | ≥15 before qualifying exam |
| PhD special track (3-year BSc) | 36 minimum | Dissertation | ≥18 before qualifying exam |

> **Note on nקודות (credit points / נ"ז):** One credit point generally equals one lecture hour per week per semester. Thesis = 20 credits; final project = 12 credits; English proficiency = 2 credits.

### Doctoral degree

The PhD is titled **דוקטור לפילוסופיה (PhD)** and is awarded by the Senate on the Dean's and Degree Committee's recommendation (sec. 31, 37.13).

## 2. Admission Requirements

### General principle (sec. 23.01, 32.01)

- Applicant must hold an appropriate (נאותה) first degree from Technion or a recognized equivalent institution.
- PhD applicants generally need an MSc (research or project track) with adequate standing.

### Grade thresholds (סף קבלה)

| Track | Minimum GPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| MSc — Technion BSc graduates | 75 weighted average | Individual units may set higher thresholds |
| MSc — equivalent institutions | Equivalent standing | Unit decision |
| PhD from Technion MSc | 80 in thesis, final exam, and coursework | Sec. 32.02 |
| Exceptional cases (sec. 23.02ב, 32.03) | Below threshold allowed | Dean approval with detailed committee reasoning |

- An applicant with GPA > 75 but below the unit's threshold who graduated ≥3 years ago may be recommended with detailed justification (sec. 23.02ג).

### Holders of a 3-year (תלת-שנתי) BSc (sec. 23.03)

- Engineering track: admitted as **סטודנט משלים** (supplementary student); must complete at least **20 credit points** of prerequisite/supplementary courses before transferring to full (מן המניין) status.
- Non-engineering track: may be admitted as מן המניין; if core courses (מקצועות ליבה) are required, enters as משלים until those are completed.

### International applicants

- Graduates of recognized foreign universities with high achievements may apply.
- 5-year program graduates may typically apply for MSc only; those who also hold a research MSc may apply for PhD.
- Specific faculties (Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Physics, Chemistry, Nano-Science, Robotics) require **GRE scores** from international applicants.
- Documents must be originals with official translation into Hebrew or English (notarized or certified by the issuing institution).

### Advisor requirement (התקשרות עם מנחה)

- Some units require a confirmed advisor before admission for the MSc research track — check unit-specific rules.
- **All PhD applicants** must have a confirmed advisor before registering.
- Registration without an advisor is possible; if no advisor is found, the candidate may transfer to another unit or to the non-thesis track (depending on unit policy).

## 3. Tuition Structure (שכר לימוד)

### Registration fees (דמי רישום) for 2025–26

- Standard fee: **₪484**
- Half fee applies to: current Technion undergraduates or recent graduates (degree received 2022–23 or later); students returning to the same track; graduate students registering for a second degree.
- Registration fees are **non-refundable**.

### Tuition for degree programs

- Tuition is set upon admission based on the total credit requirements (advanced credits + supplementary credits, minus any recognized credits).
- 100% tuition = one academic year's fee as set by the relevant authority.
- Typical total: **200%–300% of annual tuition**, depending on program.
- Non-degree track (לא לתואר): 50% of annual tuition per semester.
- Exploratory research track (מחקר גישוש): 10% of annual tuition per semester.

### Late-stage / finishing tuition

- **MSc — research/project track:** Once minimum tuition is paid and all coursework is done, 5% annual tuition per semester until thesis submission. If coursework is incomplete, 25% per semester.
- **PhD:** Once minimum tuition is paid and all coursework is done, 15% annual tuition per semester until dissertation submission.
- Students on approved late submission who miss the deadline by >3 months are charged a full semester's tuition.

### Leave / cancellation

| Timing of request | Tuition charged |
|---|---|
| Before semester start (by deadline) | None |
| Before Nov 15 (semester A) / April 15 (semester B) | 25% of semester tuition |
| After those dates | Full semester tuition |

## 4. Scholarships (מלגות)

### General principles

- Scholarships (מלגות) are awarded on **academic merit only** (sec. 3 in scholarship procedure).
- The minimum threshold is a cumulative GPA of **80** from Technion or equivalent recognized institution. This minimum must be maintained throughout the entire scholarship period — no exceptions.
- The academic unit sets the exact threshold (may be higher than 80) and may change it each semester.
- Scholarships are paid in **monthly installments (מנות מלגה)**, 1–6 per month, as determined by the unit.

### Monthly stipend amounts (2025–26, updated periodically)

| Stage | Amount per monthly unit (₪) |
|---|---|
| MSc — before approved research topic | 1,081 |
| MSc — after approved research topic | 1,156 |
| PhD — before qualifying exam (בחינת מועמדות) | 1,375 |
| PhD — after qualifying exam | 1,494 |

### Tuition waiver tied to stipend (מלגת שכר לימוד)

| Monthly units received | Tuition waiver |
|---|---|
| 1 unit | 25% |
| 2 units | 50% |
| 3 units | 75% |
| 4+ units | 100% |

The same proportional waiver applies to the annual maintenance fee (אגרת שמירה, ~₪310/year).

### Scholarship duration

| Track | Standard period | Extension possible |
|---|---|---|
| MSc | Up to 24 scholarship months | +6 months (A-6); extreme cases +6 more (B-6) |
| PhD regular track | Up to 42 months* | +12 months in two 6-month increments (O-6, A-6); extreme +6 (B-6) |
| PhD direct/special track | Up to 60 months* (from start of MSc enrollment) | Same extensions as PhD |

*In Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Medicine: 48 months for regular PhD, 66 months for direct/special.

### Residency and work restrictions during scholarship

| Monthly units | Required days on campus | Permitted outside work |
|---|---|---|
| 4+ | 5 days/week | Only as "adjunct staff" (סגל נלווה), ≤1,100 employment units |
| 3 | 4 days/week | Adjunct ≤1,400 units OR 1 day/week other work |
| 2 | 3 days/week | Adjunct ≤1,400 units OR 2 days/week other work |
| 1 | 2 days/week | Adjunct ≤1,400 units OR 3 days/week other work |

"Other work" includes participation in or membership of startups/companies, whether paid or unpaid.

### Special scholarship conditions

- **Non-thesis track:** No scholarships (except active-duty soldiers or national-service students who may apply for tuition-waiver scholarships only).
- **Supplementary (משלים) status:** Maximum 12 scholarship months; no extension.
- **Excellence bonuses (מלגת מצוינים):** Technion graduates with BSc average ≥90 who receive ≥3 monthly units get an automatic one-time bonus equal to 5 extra monthly units (paid at end of first or second semester).
- **Re-scholarship for second degree:** Permitted with documented justification from unit, dean approval, and conditions: different unit, different field, different advisor.

### Research credit accumulation requirements for scholarship holders

MSc with ≥3 monthly units:
- Must accumulate ≥75% of total required credits within first 2 semesters.
- Must study at least 8 credits in semester 1 and at least 12 in year 1.

MSc with 1–2 monthly units:
- Must accumulate ≥75% of total required credits within first 3 semesters.
- Must study at least 6 credits in semester 1 and at least 10 in year 1.

### Research topic approval deadline

MSc scholarship recipients must submit and receive approval for their research topic no later than the end of 2 semesters (or the unit's earlier deadline, per regulation 27.04).

## 5. English Language Requirement

### Requirement (sec. 26.06, 34.09)

All graduate students must demonstrate English proficiency. The specific requirement differs by degree level:

**MSc students:**
- Must pass the **בחינה באנגלית מורחבת** (Extended English Proficiency Exam) within the **first semester** of enrollment (sec. 26.06).
- If not passed in semester 1, must take the preparatory course and pass by end of semester 2.
- Failure in semester 2 → "abnormal academic standing"; one more attempt in semester 3.
- Failure in semester 3 → termination of studies.
- Passing earns **2 credit points**.

**PhD students:**
- Must pass a **scientific writing in English course** (קורס לכתיבה מדעית באנגלית) within 3 semesters of enrollment (sec. 34.09).
- Students who did not pass a dedicated English course during their MSc, and who are not exempt (see below), must first pass the Extended English exam before enrolling in the scientific writing course.

### The Extended English exam

- 3 hours; multiple-choice (American format), scanned optically.
- Tests comprehension of complex scientific/technical texts in English.
- Held three times per year: January, June, September. No makeup (מועד ב') for these exams.
- Only English–English dictionaries permitted; no bilingual or electronic dictionaries.

### Exemptions from the exam

- Holders of a PhD degree.
- Score in the 75th percentile or higher on the verbal section of GRE or GMAT.
- Graduates of high schools or recognized academic institutions where the language of instruction was English.

## 6. Research Ethics Course (אתיקה של המחקר)

### Requirement (sec. 26.07, 34.08)

- **MSc (research track):** Must pass before submitting the research topic proposal (נושא מחקר).
- **PhD:** Must pass before submitting the qualifying exam research summary (תיאור תמציתי).
- Failure to pass = prohibited from submitting the topic / summary.

### Format

- Online, self-paced course on the Technion Moodle platform.
- Exam: 21 multiple-choice questions drawn randomly from a question bank; 1 hour.
- Passing threshold: at least **18 correct answers out of 21**.
- Offered once per month, with 5 exam sessions per exam day (09:00–10:00, 11:00–12:00, 13:00–14:00, 15:00–16:00, 17:00–18:00).
- Students who fail may retry at a subsequent session (same day or following month).

## 7. MSc Regulations

### Learning tracks (נתיבי לימוד, sec. 24.01)

| Track | Hebrew | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Research | נתיב מחקר | Coursework + thesis (20 credits) + seminar lecture + final exam |
| Project (engineering) | נתיב פרויקט | Coursework + project (20 credits) + seminar lecture + final exam |
| Final project (עבודת גמר) | נתיב עבודת גמר | 28 course credits + final project (12 credits) + final exam |
| Non-thesis (ללא תזה — יחידתי) | נתיב ללא תזה | Courses only; must include project/seminar courses |
| Non-thesis (Technion-wide) | ללא תזה כלל טכניוני | Courses only |

### Credit requirements (sec. 24.02)

- **4-year BSc programs:** 38–46 advanced credits (of which 20 = thesis/project).
- **3-year BSc programs:** 50–55 advanced credits.
- Up to 10 of required credits may come from undergraduate-level courses (for interdisciplinary students, by unit decision).
- Non-thesis track (4-year BSc): minimum **42 advanced credits**.

### Cumulative GPA requirement (sec. 24.06, 26.03)

- Must maintain a GPA of at least **75** throughout studies.
- Passing grade per course: **65**.
- Failure (< 65) in a course → must retake. Second failure in same course → studies terminated (sec. 26.04).
- GPA assessed annually; if below 75, student has one year to correct. Failure to correct → termination.

### Time limits (sec. 25.01)

| Situation | Maximum semesters |
|---|---|
| 4-year BSc graduate | 8 semesters |
| 3-year BSc graduate | 10 semesters |
| 4-year BSc in a 3-year unit | 8 semesters |
| With supplementary courses | +1 semester per ~10 supplementary credits |

- Extensions: Dean may approve up to 2 years of extensions total (sec. 25.02).
- Approved leave (חופשה) does not count against the semester limit.

### Research topic and advisor (sec. 27.03–27.05)

- Research topic must be submitted and approved no later than halfway through approved study duration (for non-scholarship students) or by end of 2 semesters (scholarship students).
- Topic proposal submitted to the committee (ועדה יחידתית), approved by advisor and committee; forwarded to the Dean.
- Dean appoints the advisor (or two co-advisors). The responsible advisor must hold at least the rank of "מרצה" (lecturer) or be a PhD-holding research-track faculty member.

### Track selection deadline (sec. 24.04)

The student must declare their chosen track (from among thesis-requiring tracks) no later than halfway through the approved study period.

### Thesis defense (bechina gmar, sec. 29)

- Examining committee (ועדת בוחנים): 3+ members — advisor (chair), 1–2 senior faculty, 1 external expert (בוחן חוץ) from outside the unit.
- Reviewers must submit written opinions within 1 month of receiving the thesis.
- Oral defense; student may be required to make corrections (minor: 1 month; major: 6 months).
- First failure → allowed to re-sit within 1 year. Second failure → termination.

### Distinction grades (sec. 21.05)

- **Honors (הצטיינות):** Typically up to 15% of graduates per unit per year.
- **High honors (הצטיינות יתרה):** Up to 4% of all graduates.

## 8. PhD Regulations

### Admission (sec. 32.01–32.04)

- Requires Technion MSc (research or project track) or equivalent, with adequate standing (GPA 80+ in coursework, thesis, and final exam — sec. 32.02).
- Exceptional candidates without a standard MSc may be admitted with detailed committee justification and dean approval.
- Candidates with wide prerequisite requirements enter as **סטודנט משלים** until conditions are met.

### Credit requirements (sec. 34.02)

- Minimum: **5 advanced credits** in formal coursework (set by the unit committee).
- Special track (מסלול מיוחד, 4-year BSc): ≥25 credits, with ≥15 before qualifying exam.
- Special track (3-year BSc): ≥36 credits, with ≥18 before qualifying exam.
- Direct-track (מסלול ישיר): must complete MSc-level credit requirements in addition.

### Academic standing (sec. 34.05)

- Minimum GPA: **75** (assessed annually by the committee).
- Research progress assessed every semester based on advisor report; minimum "satisfactory" rating.
- Below standard for one year → required to improve. Failure to improve → termination.

### Time limits (sec. 33.01)

- Maximum: **12 semesters** (plus extensions up to 2 years).

### Qualifying exam — בחינת מועמדות (sec. 36)

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| Research summary (תיאור תמציתי) | ~25 pages submitted via advisor; includes title (He+En), background, literature review, research objectives |
| Submission deadline | Within 11 months of full-status acceptance (18 months for special track) |
| Exam timing | ~1 month after submitting the summary |
| Purpose | Assess candidate's approach to the problem, mastery of background and literature, suitability for PhD |
| Committee | ≥5 members: advisor, co-advisor (if any), ≥4 additional senior faculty (at least 1 external from a different unit) |
| Outcome | Pass / retake / (for direct/special track) return to MSc / termination |
| Retake | Allowed after 1–2 semesters; second failure → termination |
| Late submission | Treated as failure if submitted without prior approval |

### Seminar lecture (sec. 35.05)

- Must be given in the final year of studies, no later than 2 weeks before thesis submission.
- Publicly announced at the Technion.

### Dissertation defense (sec. 37)

- Examining committee: ≥3 members — advisor (chair), ≥1 senior faculty (associate professor or higher), 1 external expert from outside Technion.
- Reviewers submit written opinions within **2 months** of receiving the dissertation.
- Oral defense; corrections if required (minor: 1 month; major: 6 months).
- First failure → re-sit within 1 year. Second failure → termination.

## 9. Direct PhD Track (מסלול ישיר לדוקטורט, sec. 24.07, 32.06)

The direct track allows an exceptional MSc student to transfer directly to PhD without completing the MSc first.

### Eligibility (sec. 24.07)

Standard conditions:
1. Completed at least **1 full semester** after research topic approval.
2. Completed at least **half of required course credits** with GPA ≥ **90**.
3. Committee is convinced the MSc research topic can be extended to PhD scope; recommends the transfer.

Expedited conditions (for top graduates):
- Technion 4-year BSc with honors (GPA ≥85 or last-4-semester president's honor): may apply after 1 semester, having completed ≥1/3 of required credits (minimum 8).
- Technion/university 3-year BSc with highest honors (president's honor or equivalent): same relaxed conditions.

### Process after transfer approval

- Student status converted to PhD.
- Study period extended by 8 more semesters.
- Must complete ≥5 additional advanced credits.
- Must submit qualifying exam summary and pass the qualifying exam within 19 months from start of MSc enrollment, or within 6 months of the transfer notification (whichever applies per sec. 36.01).
- Upon passing the qualifying exam and completing all required MSc-level courses, the student is entitled to an MSc certificate (sec. 21.04).
- If the student fails the qualifying exam, they are returned to their previous MSc status (not terminated).

### Scholarship duration for direct/special track

- Up to **60 months** (from start of MSc enrollment); 66 months in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Medicine.

## 10. Academic Standing and Dismissal Rules

### Course failure (sec. 26.04, 34.06)

- A grade below **65** is a failure.
- Student must re-register and retake the course.
- A student may retake a failed course **once**. Second failure in the same course → studies terminated.
- From academic year 2018–19 onward: students may sit exam sessions A and B; improvement attempts allowed.

### GPA-based standing (sec. 26.03, 34.05)

- **MSc:** GPA < 75 at year-end → warning; failure to reach 75 the following year → termination.
- **PhD:** Same threshold; additionally, unsatisfactory research progress (assessed per semester) for two consecutive semesters → termination.
- The Dean may allow continuation in exceptional cases with documented committee and advisor recommendation.

### Inactivity (sec. 25.06)

- Dean may terminate enrollment of a student who has not registered for courses and has no approved research topic.

### Scholarship freezing / termination (sec. 5 of scholarship procedure)

The Dean may freeze or permanently terminate a scholarship without notice if the student:
- Voluntarily stops studies;
- Is dismissed due to academic failure;
- Violates terms of the scholarship agreement;
- Is charged in a disciplinary proceeding.

### Return after dismissal (sec. 23.08, 32.11)

- If studies were stopped for non-disciplinary reasons, the student may reapply; committee may recognize previous work.
- If stopped for disciplinary reasons, readmission requires a disciplinary court ruling.
- Readmission requires settlement of any outstanding financial obligations.

## 11. Leave of Absence Rules

### Who may take leave (sec. 25.05, 33.05)

A student who **cannot** continue studies must submit a leave request no later than **14 days from the start of the semester**, with justification and signed approval from advisor and committee chair.

Eligible students (sec. from the administrative guide):
- Students in good academic standing: GPA ≥ 75, English requirement met.
- Up to **2 semesters** of leave during the entire degree.

### Who may NOT take leave

- Students in **משלים** (supplementary) status who have not yet transferred to מן המניין.
- Students whose original approved study period has ended.
- Students in an extension period.
- PhD students who have not yet passed the qualifying exam.
- Students currently receiving a scholarship (except rare humanitarian cases).
- Students who received the full scholarship allotment and have not yet submitted the thesis.

### Duration

- The Dean may grant 1–2 semesters of leave; in exceptional cases up to 2 years.
- A student who does not return by the end of the approved leave is considered to have voluntarily stopped studies.

### Approved leave and tuition

- Leave approved **before** the semester start: no tuition charged for that semester.
- Late request (after semester start, before Nov 15 / Apr 15): 25% tuition charged.
- Request after those dates: full semester tuition charged.

## 12. Exploratory Research and Dual-Institution PhD

### Exploratory research (מחקר גישוש, sec. in administrative guide)

MSc graduates (from Technion or other institution with special Dean approval) who completed a **non-thesis MSc** and wish to pursue a PhD may apply for a "לא לתואר" exploratory research period:
- Must have excelled in their MSc.
- BSc achievements must meet the unit's MSc admission threshold.
- Research topic must be in the intended PhD area; same advisor for both.
- Duration: up to 2 semesters; tuition = 10% of annual tuition per semester.
- A committee (advisor + 2 additional reviewers) evaluates the exploratory research.
- Scholarship may be awarded during this period per unit recommendation.

### Joint/dual PhD (דוקטורט דו-מוסדי)

- The Technion allows a joint PhD dissertation supervised by both a Technion faculty member and a supervisor at a partnered foreign university.
- Upon completion, the student receives a degree certificate from each institution.
- Regulated under the "joint PhD" procedure: https://graduate.technion.ac.il/potential_candidates/joint-phd/

### 3. שכר לימוד

> "כל סטודנט לתואר מגיסטר ולתואר דוקטור ישלם שכר לימוד לפי התנאים שהיו רשומים בפרסומי בית הספר, שהיו בתוקף בעת קבלתו."
