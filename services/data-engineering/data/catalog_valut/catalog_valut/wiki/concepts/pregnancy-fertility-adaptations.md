# Pregnancy, Childbirth, and Fertility Adaptations (Technion)

The Technion is required by **Section 19b of the Student Rights Law (5767-2007)** and the **Student Rights Rules (Adaptations due to Fertility Treatments, Pregnancy, Childbirth, Adoption, or Foster/Intended Parenthood), 5772-2012** to establish and publish adaptation procedures. The internal Technion procedure was approved by the Standing Committee for Academic Studies on 24 July 2012, 25 November 2012, 9 November 2014, and 5 May 2019.

> "The procedure is written in the feminine form but applies equally to men and women; feminine language also implies masculine and vice versa."

---

## Legal Basis

| Instrument | Content |
|------------|---------|
| Student Rights Law §19b | Mandates each institution to set and publish adaptation rules for fertility treatments, pregnancy, childbirth, adoption, and foster/intended parenthood |
| Student Rights Rules (Fertility/Pregnancy/Childbirth), 5772-2012 | National-level minimum adaptation entitlements |
| Technion Internal Procedure (תשע"ג) | Full Technion-specific implementation rules |

## Adaptations Coordinator (רכזת התאמות)

The **Dean of Students (דיקנית הסטודנטים)**, or a designee, serves as **Adaptations Coordinator**. The position preferentially involves a woman. The Coordinator's name and contact details are published in the annual admissions booklet and on the Technion website.

### Coordinator's Responsibilities

- Manage all matters related to the procedure
- Receive and process student eligibility requests
- Issue **Eligibility Certificates (אישור זכאות)** specifying the period of qualifying absence and the entitlements
- Publish relevant information (in coordination with the Deans; where possible, after Student Union consultation)
- Advise and guide students
- Refer eligible students to the Undergraduate Secretariat (for B.Sc. students) or the Graduate School (for advanced-degree students) for implementation
- Handle complaints regarding adaptation implementation
- Report annually (statistically only) to the Senior Vice-President; statistical summary forwarded to the Student Union
- Maintain confidentiality of all medical/personal documents submitted

## Qualifying Events ("אירוע מזכה")

An absence qualifies for adaptations if it amounts to **at least 21 calendar days** within a single semester due to any of the following:

| Event | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| Pregnancy bed rest (שמירת היריון) | Medical-ordered absence during pregnancy |
| Post-birth leave (חופשה לאחר לידה) | Period immediately following childbirth |
| Adoption travel (נסיעה לאימוץ בין-ארצי) | Travel abroad for international adoption under §28a of the Adoption of Children Law 5741-1981 |
| Fertility treatment (טיפולי פוריות) | Absence for fertility treatment sessions |
| Post-adoption/foster care leave | Period following receipt of a child as an intended parent or foster parent |

A qualifying absence must be documented by a **signed written confirmation from a professional medical and/or social authority**, to the satisfaction of the Adaptations Coordinator.

---

## How to Apply

1. Submit the **Request Form (נספח א')** to the Adaptations Coordinator, signed by the student, specifying: the absence period, the reason (checked from the list above), and any requested specific adaptations
2. Attach medical/social certificates confirming the qualifying absence
3. The Coordinator may request additional information; may consult the Technion physician
4. The Coordinator decides within **10 working days** of receiving all required materials
5. If approved, the student receives an **Eligibility Certificate (נספח ב')** that does not state the reason for absence — only that the student was absent for a qualifying event
6. The student presents the certificate to the relevant Secretariat/personnel, who must honor it **without requesting the underlying medical documentation**
7. Upon returning from a qualifying absence, the student must **report their return** in writing to the Adaptations Coordinator and to the Undergraduate or Graduate Secretariat of their academic unit

## Adaptation Entitlements

### 1. Absence from Classes (§6.1)

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| General right | Eligible student may miss up to **30% of mandatory-attendance classes** per course |
| Overlap rule | This approved absence is counted together with other permitted absences (from any cause); total approved absence from all causes must not exceed 30% |
| Post-childbirth special rule | For a student after giving birth, the permitted absence is **six weeks OR 30% of mandatory-attendance classes, whichever is greater** (Standing Committee decision, 9 November 2014) |
| Partner/spouse post-birth right | A male student (or female partner) after childbirth, adoption, or foster placement is entitled to an **additional 10%** absence from mandatory-attendance classes in every course during the infant's **first year of life** |

### 2. Course Deferral (§6.2)

An eligible student may **defer (retake without additional payment)** a course she was registered in during that semester to a later semester, provided she has not yet been examined in the course, and subject to the course being offered again.

### 3. Assignments / Papers (§6.3)

An eligible student who was absent near a submission deadline may submit the assignment or an **alternative assignment (as determined by the lecturer)** after returning, with a deadline of **seven weeks from the date of return**.

### 4. Practical Training (מעבדות, סדנאות וכד') (§6.4)

| Step | Rule |
|------|------|
| Preferred | Complete the missed practical training in a **parallel session** (same content, different group or time) within the same semester |
| If no parallel session | Complete a **similar** session (not identical content but close enough to satisfy the academic objectives, per the responsible lecturer) — same semester or another time set by the lecturer |
| If no similar session exists | Dean of Undergraduate Studies or Dean of Graduate Studies considers exempting the student from the practical training, after consulting the head of the relevant academic unit |
| If none of the above | Student completes the practical training at the next available opportunity |

### 5. Exam Adaptations (§6.5)

| Scenario | Entitlement |
|----------|-------------|
| Absent from an exam due to a qualifying event | **Additional exam date** within the framework of standard Technion exam dates |
| Absent from an exam within **15 weeks after childbirth**, adoption, or foster placement | Additional exam date within standard dates OR another date approved by the Dean (after the Adaptations Coordinator's and lecturer's recommendation) |
| **Pregnant student** — bathroom break during an exam of 2+ hours (normally prohibited) | **Entitled** to leave for the bathroom during the exam (accompanied by an invigilator, staggered exit if multiple students leave) |
| **Pregnant student** — exam time extension | **25% additional time** (this overlaps partially with other time extensions the student may be entitled to, so total extension may not exceed **30%** of exam duration; this provision overrides other provisions unless explicitly stated otherwise) |
| **Student within 15 weeks post-childbirth** | **25% additional time** on all written exams and on MD final exams (if also entitled to additional time from another reason, total extension may not exceed 25%) |

### 6. Absence from Prerequisite Exam / Task (§6.6)

An eligible student who missed an exam or failed to submit a task in a **prerequisite course** due to a qualifying event is entitled to **study conditionally** (על-תנאי) in the advanced course or year until completing the exam or task.

### 7. Study Extension (§6.7)

An eligible student is entitled to extend her studies by **2 semesters** beyond the original allocated period, without being charged additional tuition or mandatory fees. (Note: payments for optional services such as dormitories are not considered mandatory fees.)

### 8. Campus Parking (§6.8)

A student in **7th month or later of pregnancy** is entitled to a **free red parking permit**, limited to half a year.

### 9. Research Scholarship (Graduate Students) (§6.9)

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| During qualifying absence | Scholarship is **not suspended** for up to **15 weeks** |
| After return | Scholarship eligibility period is **extended** by a period equal to the qualifying absence, up to 15 weeks, provided the student notified the Technion of the qualifying event within **30 days** of it occurring |
| If scholarship eligibility had already ended | Extension is calculated only for the months during which scholarship was being paid |
| Extended post-birth leave (beyond 15 weeks) | If additional leave beyond the mandatory 15 weeks is approved, scholarship eligibility is **not** extended for the additional leave period |

To request a scholarship extension beyond the 15-week period (up to 14 additional weeks without scholarship), the student must apply directly to the Graduate Studies Secretariat in the academic unit.

### 10. Partner/Spouse Adaptations (§6.10)

| Scenario | Entitlement |
|----------|-------------|
| Partner of someone experiencing a qualifying event (even if the qualifying-event person is not a Technion student) | Exemption from mandatory class attendance for **one week**, subject to the Coordinator being satisfied that the qualifying event is the reason for the absence |
| Partner/spouse of a woman who gave birth, who missed an exam held within **three weeks of the birth date** | Entitled to **an additional exam date** within standard Technion exam dates |

### 11. Excellence Awards and Prizes (§6.11)

Prizes, scholarships, and excellence awards from the Technion whose eligibility criterion is the length of the study period **count the qualifying absence period** in calculating eligibility.

### 12. Photocopy Credits (§6.12)

An eligible student who missed classes is entitled to **20 photocopies or prints of study materials per day of absence**.

## Infrastructure and Facility Adaptations (Chapter 7)

### Laboratory Safety (§7.1)

The Technion publishes on its safety unit website a list of substances used in Technion labs that may pose risks to pregnant or nursing women. A **pregnant or nursing student is obligated to report** her status to the lab supervisor and to check whether any such hazardous substances are present. If she cannot participate in a lab or other practical training due to such risk, the rules of §6.4 (practical training adaptations) apply.

### Room and Building Adaptations (§7.2)

- At least **one nursing room** per academic unit, equipped with comfortable seating, electrical outlet, refrigerator, changing mat, and nearby sink
- At least **one toilet with a changing mat** per academic unit or main building, marked with standard signage
- The locations of nursing rooms and changing mats are published on the Technion website
- A pregnant student who needs a special seating arrangement should contact the Adaptations Coordinator

## Additional Adaptations for Non-Qualifying Absences (Chapter 8)

Even if a student's absence does not meet the 21-day threshold for a "qualifying event," the Adaptations Coordinator may recommend additional adaptations to the Dean of Undergraduate Studies or Dean of Graduate Studies after consulting with the lecturer, if the student's studies were affected by fertility treatments, pregnancy, childbirth, adoption, or foster/intended parenthood.

---

## Application Forms

The procedure includes two official forms:

**Appendix A — Request Form (נספח א'):** Submitted by the student to the Adaptations Coordinator, specifying the qualifying event type, absence dates, and the specific adaptations requested from the following list:
- Absence from classes (§6.1)
- Course deferral (§6.2)
- Assignments (§6.3)
- Practical training (§6.4)
- Exam adaptations (§6.5)
- Absence from prerequisite exam/task (§6.6)
- Study extension (§6.7) — 1 or 2 semesters (circle)
- Campus parking (§6.8)
- Research scholarship — graduate students only (§6.9)
- Partner adaptations (§6.10)
- Excellence awards (§6.11)
- Photocopy credits (§6.12)

**Appendix B — Eligibility Certificate (נספח ב'):** Issued by the Adaptations Coordinator to the student, specifying the certified absence dates and the approved adaptations. The certificate does not state the reason for the absence.

## נתונים בעברית

### סעיף 19ב לחוק זכויות הסטודנט, תשס"ז-2007

(א) מוסד יקבע הוראות בדבר התאמות שיינתנו לסטודנטים ולסטודנטיות עקב טיפולי פוריות, היריון, לידה, אימוץ או קבלת ילד למשמורת כהורה מיועד או כהורה במשפחת אומנה.

### מנוהל בדבר התאמות עקב טיפולי פוריות, הריון, לידה, אימוץ או קבלת ילד למשמורת או אומנה (טכניון, תשע"ג):

**הגדרות:**

"היעדרות בשל אירוע מזכה": היעדרות לתקופה של 21 ימים (קלנדרית) לפחות, במהלך סמסטר, בשל אחד מן האירועים המנויים להלן, ובלבד שהומצא, ביחס אליה, אישור בכתב חתום על ידי גורם מקצועי - רפואי ו/או סוציאלי, להנחת דעת רכזת ההתאמות:
- היעדרות לצורך שמירת היריון.
- היעדרות בתקופה הסמוכה לאחר לידה (חופשה לאחר לידה), אימוץ או קבלת ילד למשמורת כהורה מיועד או כהורה במשפחת אומנה.
- היעדרות לצורך נסיעה למדינת חוץ למטרת אימוץ בין-ארצי כמשמעותו בסעיף 28א לחוק אימוץ ילדים, התשמ"א-1981.
- היעדרות לצורך קבלת טיפולי פוריות.

**§6.1.3 – לאחר לידה:**
למרות האמור לעיל, תקופת ההיעדרות המותרת עבור סטודנטית לאחר לידה, תעמוד על שישה שבועות או 30% מכלל השיעורים במקצוע שחלה בו חובת נוכחות – לפי הגבוה (ועדת הקבע ללימודים אקדמיים 9.11.2014).

**§6.1.4 – בן/בת זוג לאחר לידה:**
סטודנט לאחר לידה, אימוץ או קבלת ילד למשמורת או אומנה, יהיה זכאי להיעדרות נוספת של 10% מכלל השיעורים בכל קורס שחלה בו חובת נוכחות בעבור טיפול בתינוק בשנת חייו הראשונה.

**§6.5.3 – הריון ושירותים בבחינה:**
על אף האמור בסעיף 6.7 לנספח ד׳ של תקנות ונהלים בבית הספר ללימודי הסמכה (העוסק ביציאה לשירותים בבחינות בנות שעתיים ויותר), סטודנטית בהיריון זכאית לצאת לשירותים במהלך בחינה. היציאה לשירותים תהיה באישור המשגיח ובהתאם להוראותיו.

**§6.5.4 – תוספת זמן בהריון:**
סטודנטית בהיריון זכאית לתוספת זמן בבחינה בשיעור של 25% ממשך זמן הבחינה. תוספת זמן זו חופפת, באופן חלקי, לתוספות זמן אחרות להן זכאית הסטודנטית, ככל שהיא זכאית, באופן שסך תוספת זמן הארכות לא יעלה על 30% ממשך זמן הבחינה.

**§6.5.5 – תוספת זמן לאחר לידה:**
סטודנטית בתקופה של 15 שבועות לאחר לידה זכאית לתוספת של 25% בבחינות בכתב בכלל המקצועות וכן בבחינות הגמר ברפואה (MD). במקרה שבו זכאית סטודנטית לתוספת זמן גם מסיבה אחרת, שיעור התוספת הכולל לא יעלה על 25%.

**§6.9.1 – מלגה למחקר:**
סטודנטית שנעדרת מלימודיה בשל אירוע מזכה, לא תופסק מלגתה, לתקופה של עד 15 שבועות.

**§6.10.2 – בן/בת זוג שנעדר/ה מבחינה:**
בן זוג או בת זוג של יולדת לאחר לידה שנעדר/ה מבחינה שנערכה במהלך שלושה שבועות מיום הלידה, יהיה זכאי/תהיה זכאית למועד בחינה נוסף במסגרת המועדים המקובלים לבחינות בטכניון.

**§6.8 – חניה בקמפוס:**
סטודנטית הנמצאת בחודש השביעי ואילך להריונה, תהיה זכאית לקבל תו חניה (אדום) ללא תשלום. התו יוגבל לחצי שנה.
