# Combined Biomedical Engineering and Physics BSc Track

**Faculty:** [[faculty-biomedical-engineering]]
**Program Code:** 033133-1-000
**Degree:** מוסמך למדעים בהנדסה ביו-רפואית ובפיזיקה (BSc in Biomedical Engineering and Physics)
**Duration:** 4 years (8 semesters)
**Total Credits:** 178.0

## Motivation

This track is designed for students who want to develop both scientific-technological knowledge and deeper physical understanding of phenomena and modern research tools in biomedical engineering. The goal is to train engineers/scientists with in-depth knowledge in both biomedical engineering and physics.

This combined view is increasingly required at the frontier of:
- Nano-biotechnology development
- Biomedical instrumentation relying on complex physical phenomena
- Biomedical optics
- Nuclear and magnetic imaging (MRI)
- Nano-medicine
- Advanced research topics based on experimental and theoretical tools from physics

## Elective Requirements (Faculty Electives — 22.5 credits)

- Minimum **22.5 credits** total in faculty electives from **both faculties**: Biomedical Engineering and Physics (see elective lists from both faculties)
- Must complete at least **one elective group** from BME domain areas per group rules
- At least **5 of the elective courses** must come from the Faculty of Biomedical Engineering (course codes 33x0xxx)
- **Special rule (this track only):** Courses 3350002 BME Lab 2 and 3360022 From Cell to Tissue also count as faculty electives in this track

⁵ Faculty elective rules: must draw from both BME and Physics elective lists. At least one BME elective group required.

## Special Rules

1. Some courses are offered **once a year** forming prerequisite chains — follow recommended plan to avoid delays.
2. Study plan may change — students must update themselves at the start of each academic year.
3. Technical English Advanced B is counted in mandatory credits. Required English courses must be completed by end of Semester 4.
