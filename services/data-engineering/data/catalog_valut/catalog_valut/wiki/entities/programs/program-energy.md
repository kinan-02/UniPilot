# Energy — Interdisciplinary Program (Technion)

**Program code:** 510
**Degrees granted:** Multiple tracks (see below)
**Level:** Graduate (Master's and Doctorate)

## Participating Faculties

Architecture and Town Planning, Biology, Civil and Environmental Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, Biotechnology and Food Engineering, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Chemistry, Materials Science and Engineering, Computer Science, Physics.

## Program Overview

The program addresses the critical global need for clean and renewable energy sources, replacing fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas). Technion established this interdisciplinary research program to support a wide range of activities including: alternative fuels, energy storage and conversion, renewable energy sources, and energy conservation.

The program's vision is to train the engineering and scientific workforce that will lead the energy field in Israel and globally — in academia, industry, and at key decision-making points. Future energy engineers and scientists will need broad understanding across classical engineering and science disciplines, as well as entrepreneurship, economics, and policy.

## Admission Requirements

Open to students meeting the Graduate School's admission requirements — graduates of engineering or science faculties. Key requirements:
- Must find a supervisor from among the faculty members of the participating units (joint supervision is encouraged)
- Submit a shortened research proposal
- Approval of the candidate and research topic by the program committee
- Research topics must incorporate scientific/engineering aspects of energy
- Two letters of recommendation from lecturers
- Series of personal interviews

Candidates with a bachelor's degree and extensive relevant work experience (~5 years) may apply with a detailed application, CV, and two letters of recommendation (from employers and/or lecturers), subject to the interdisciplinary committee's assessment.

### Doctorate (PhD)

**Admission:** Holders of a research master's degree in engineering or exact sciences with excellent prior academic and research achievements, and demonstrated ability to conduct independent research.

Requirements for PhD candidates:
- Find a supervisor from program faculty before applying
- Submit a preliminary research proposal (2 pages in English)
- Submit CV and two letters of recommendation (one from MSc thesis supervisor)

**Study requirements:**
- Fulfil any special conditions imposed by the interdisciplinary committee
- At least **8 advanced course credits**
- Submit and defend a research proposal before an examining committee
- Deliver a seminar lecture announced at Technion
- Submit and defend a dissertation before an examining committee

**Direct/Combined Doctoral Track:** Outstanding students who began an MSc with thesis and excel in studies and research may transfer to the direct doctoral track (at the committee's recommendation). They will be required to complete at least 8 additional advanced credits beyond MSc requirements.

**Special fast-track for excellence:** Designed for students graduating with distinction (הצטיינות יתרה) directly from undergraduate studies. Admission and study procedures detailed in the Graduate School catalog (sections 32.05 and 34.02).

## Special Notes

- Course registration requires supervisor recommendation and approval of the interdisciplinary energy studies committee
- Research work (20 credits) must be conducted within one of the participating academic units, or jointly across two or more units
- Research topic must encompass both energy science and engineering aspects, approved by the committee
