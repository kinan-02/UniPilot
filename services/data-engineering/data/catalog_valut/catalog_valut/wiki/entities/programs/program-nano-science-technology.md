## Program Overview

The program operates within an interdisciplinary committee comprising the above faculties. Science related to nanometric systems and the technology derived from it combines knowledge from different fields that, within a university framework, are studied and researched in separate faculties.

In recent years there has been a significant scientific surge, followed by technological development manifested in the application of nanotechnology in many areas: nano-particles are used in medicine — in diagnostics and treatment; advanced technologies apply nanotechnology in fields ranging from optoelectronic devices to water treatment.

Technion places high priority on the development of research and teaching in nano-science and nano-technology, and invests significant resources. The **Russell Berrie Nanotechnology Institute** was established, and there are significant collaborations among researchers from different disciplines studying the scientific aspects of organization and functionality in natural and synthetic systems at the nanometric scale, and applying these principles in technological development.

Any Technion faculty member is a potential supervisor for an advanced degree within this program. The program allows study for an advanced degree even if the student holds a prior degree from a different field or faculty than that of the supervisor.

## Admission Requirements

### Master's (MSc)

Intended for outstanding students with a bachelor's degree with background in one of the following fields: Electrical and Computer Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Biotechnology and Food Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Medicine, Materials Science and Engineering.

- Minimum GPA: **88** or above
- The program committee assesses admission based on, among other things, GPA in prior studies, letters of recommendation, and interviews with representatives of the interdisciplinary committee
- Candidates who studied their previous degree at a university abroad must take the **GRE** and indicate the score on the application form

### Doctorate (PhD)

Candidates must hold an MSc in Natural Sciences from Technion (or an equivalent degree from another recognized academic institution) with appropriate background and a GPA of **90 or above** in both courses and thesis.

Outstanding MSc students (90+) with excellence in research may transfer to the direct doctoral track in accordance with Technion regulations.

## Program Structure and Credit Requirements

### Master's (MSc)

**4-year bachelor's graduates:**
- 17 advanced course credits
- 2 credits — Extended English (אנגלית מורחבת)
- 20 credits — Research thesis
- **Total: 39 credits**

**3-year bachelor's graduates:**
- At least 20 of 30 course credits must be at advanced graduate level
- 2 credits — Extended English
- 20 credits — Research thesis
- **Total: 52 credits**

All courses are selected by the supervisor and student according to the student's research area, subject to the program's approval. Language requirements follow Graduate School regulations.

### Research

Research work is carried out for 20 credits. Research topics are approved by the program committee in accordance with Graduate School regulations. The interdisciplinary committee encourages interdisciplinary research programs and joint supervision by faculty from different faculties.

### Doctorate (PhD)

- 9 advanced course credits
- All courses selected by supervisor and student per research area, with program approval

### Required Application Documents

**For MSc:**
- Name of supervisor (after obtaining their consent)
- Two academic letters of recommendation (sent directly to the program secretariat)
- CV
- Official signed transcripts from prior academic degrees
- Degree completion certificates from prior academic degrees
- Madrag (ranking) — only for non-Technion graduates
- Identification documents as required by Graduate School

**For PhD:**
- Name of supervisor (after obtaining their consent)
- Two academic letters of recommendation (sent directly to the program secretariat)
- CV
- Official signed transcripts from prior academic degrees
- Degree completion certificates from prior academic degrees
- Identification documents as required by Graduate School

## Special Notes

- The program enables study for an advanced degree even when the student's prior degree is from a different field or faculty than the supervisor's
- Joint supervision by faculty from multiple faculties is actively encouraged
- Unique feature: any Technion faculty member can serve as a supervisor, crossing faculty boundaries
