## Program Overview

Autonomous systems represent one of the next great steps in engineering. These systems combine a wide range of sensors, computers, and various communication means, and constitute a significant part of the frontier of technology, affecting all areas of engineering. These systems perform tasks — including responses — dynamically to changing conditions and unexpected events occurring in real time in the real world.

Examples of autonomous systems include: unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), unmanned ground and marine vehicles, medical assistive tools and systems, and autonomous agent systems in various domains.

The program's goal is to concentrate the variety of teaching, research, and development capabilities in autonomous systems.

## Admission Requirements

Candidates meeting the Graduate School's admission requirements may be accepted in all tracks (MSc research/project, ME without thesis, and PhD). Specific admission requirements are at minimum those of the planned supervisor's home unit, but no less than a grade of **85**.

The program's admissions committee will invite candidates for a personal interview, at its discretion, and will determine individual admission requirements.

**MSc (research/project track):** Must find a supervisor/supervisors for the research/project work, and submit a concise research proposal within **one semester** for committee approval. Failure to meet this condition results in termination of studies.

**PhD and international master's students:** Must find a supervisor and submit a research topic upon registration.

## Program Structure and Credit Requirements

### MSc — Research or Project Track (with thesis or project)

**4-year engineering graduates:**
- At least **20 credits** from advanced courses
- 2 credits — Extended English (אנגלית מורחבת)
- 20 credits — Research thesis / project
- **Total: 42 credits minimum**

### ME — Without Thesis

**4-year engineering graduates:**
- **40 credits** from advanced courses
- 2 credits — Extended English
- **Total: 42 credits**

### 3-year program graduates (all tracks)

An additional **20 credits** of supplementary/prerequisite courses are required. During the supplementary period, the student holds "completing student" (משלים) status. Additional supplementary requirements may be imposed based on the candidate's background.

### Course Selection Requirements

Every student must study courses from Lists A, B, and C, including:
- At least **3 courses** from List A (or a total of **8 credits**)
- At least **1 course** from List C
- Additional courses required for the degree are determined by the supervisor(s)

### Doctorate (PhD)

**Study requirements:**
- At least **3 courses** (minimum 9 credits), determined by the supervisor's recommendation and student's background, approved by the program's graduate studies committee

**Research Proposal:**
Approved by the program's graduate studies committee after the supervisor's agreement.

## Research Proposal (Master's)

The research proposal is approved by the program's graduate studies committee after the supervisor's agreement.
