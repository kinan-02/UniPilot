# Faculty of Biomedical Engineering (Technion)

**Dean:** Prof. Joshua Shnitman (שניטמן ג'וזואה)
**Graduate Studies Office Contact:** bm.g.ad@technion.ac.il | 073-3784130
**Faculty Number (for course codes):** 33

## Overview

The Faculty of Biomedical Engineering is a multidisciplinary unit applying engineering knowledge and tools to develop medical diagnosis and treatment methods, and to investigate the physiological basis of diseases. Biomedical engineering spans areas including:
- Artificial organs and implants
- Medical devices for diagnosis and treatment
- Catheterization and medical imaging equipment
- Rehabilitation engineering and assistive devices for people with disabilities
- Biochemical engineering
- Tissue engineering
- Brain rehabilitation

The Israeli and global biomedical industry is knowledge-intensive, export-oriented, and characterized by innovation, sophistication, and continuous pursuit of new challenges.

Graduates of the faculty integrate into the biomedical industry and other industries, occupying key roles in development and production, marketing, and health services application, as well as in numerous startup companies.

### MSc in Biomedical Engineering — Requirements
- Total credits: **40**
  - 18 credits from advanced-level courses
  - 2 credits for Extended English (אנגלית מורחבת)
  - 20 credits for research thesis
- BME Technion graduates: minimum 6 credits from faculty courses
- Non-BME faculty graduates: minimum 9 credits from faculty courses, including Physiology of Body Systems for Engineers (3370002)
- Admission GPA (BME Technion graduates): 83+ as full student; 82–83 conditional
- Admission GPA (engineering faculty graduates): 84+
- Admission GPA (exact sciences, 4-year): 84+
- Admission GPA (life sciences, 4-year or 3-year): 87+

### MSc (without faculty name) — Requirements
- For students from a substantially different prior field
- 3-year degree holders: minimum 52 credits

### ME in Biomedical Engineering (without thesis) — Requirements
- Total credits: **42** coursework
- 2 credits for Extended English
- Submission of a final project
- BME Technion graduates: minimum 13 credits from faculty courses
- Non-BME faculty graduates: minimum 20 credits from faculty courses, including Physiology of Body Systems for Engineers (3370002)
- Admission criteria identical to MSc

### PhD — Requirements
- Total: 9 credits + research thesis
- Candidate must generally hold an MSc with very good record
- Minimum GPA in MSc studies: 85; thesis grade: above 85
- Candidacy exam: submit research proposal within 11 months of start
- Students who completed ME (without thesis) may be considered for PhD if they complete research work under a non-degree framework

## Special Programs and Features

- **Clinical-Engineering Project (פרויקט קליני-הנדסי):** Students work in clinical departments of hospitals, encountering clinical environments and identifying engineering needs that require solutions.
- **Entrepreneurial Project (פרויקט יזמי):** Part of the secondary specialization in entrepreneurial leadership.
- **Advanced Lab (מעבדה מתקדמת 1 & 2):** Courses 3340019 and 3340020 take place in the faculty's research labs. One counts as general faculty elective, the other as free elective.
- **Biodesign Innovation Model (חדשנות רפואית במודל ביודיזיין 1+2):** Courses 3360024 and 3360025 can replace Project in Biomedical Engineering 1+2.
- **Research Excellence Project (פרויקט מחקרי למצטיינים):** Course 3340305.
- **Industry Encounters (מפגשים עם התעשיה הביו-רפואית):** Course 3340331, offered as faculty elective (semester 5 recommendation) or as free elective.

## Graduate Research Areas

Main research areas include:
- **Biomechanics and Fluid Flow** — biomechanics of molecules, cells, tissues, organs; blood circulation; micro-flow; artificial organs; rehabilitation biomechanics
- **Molecular and Cellular Medicine** — single-molecule sensors; genomics/proteomics at single-cell level; cell imaging; nanoparticles; gene expression control
- **Biological Signals and Systems** — medical signal and image processing; opto-physiology; medical device design; prosthetics; deep learning in physiological analysis
- **Tissue Engineering and Bio-Materials** — stem cells; bio-rheology; mechano-biology; bio-compatible implants; controlled drug delivery; lab-on-chip
- **Brain Research and Rehabilitation Engineering** — neural interfaces; neural computation; brain-motor system research; brain injury and spinal cord rehabilitation
- **Cardiovascular System** — cardiac electro-mechanical coupling; blood flow; cardiac imaging; arrhythmia; cardiac pacemakers
- **Cancer Diagnosis and Treatment Technologies** — nanoparticles for imaging/treatment; cancer cell mechanics; metastasis; cancer-specific device systems
- **Medical Biophysics** — single-molecule characterization; nanobiophysics; nanopore sequencing
- **Nano-Medicine Engineering** — self-assembly predictions; targeted drug delivery; fluorescent quantum dot stabilizers
- **Medical Imaging** — US, CT, PET, MRI; therapeutic ultrasound; bio-photonics; deep learning for MRI
- **Nuclear Research and Applications** — genome organization; protein-DNA interactions; structure-function in the cell nucleus
